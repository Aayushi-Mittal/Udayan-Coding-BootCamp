Udayan Care

Udayan is a Sanskrit word meaning 'Eternal Sunrise'. Udayan Care aims to bring sunshine into the lives of underserved sections of society that require intervention. Registered in 1994 as a Public Charitable Trust, Udayan Care works to empower vulnerable children, women and youth, in 25 cities across 14 states of India.

We started with the Udayan Ghar Programme for orphaned and abandoned children. In 2002, we moved towards higher education for girls through the Udayan Shalini Fellowships. In 2004, we started the Udayan Information and Technology Centres to improve employability of underserved communities.
